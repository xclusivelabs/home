What is DigiMacroPad?

DigiMacroPad is a compact USB device that puts your most-used shortcuts at your fingertips. Assign any keyboard shortcut — copy, paste, undo, screenshot, or anything you use daily — to a dedicated button and trigger it instantly without hunting through menus or remembering key combos.

With 10 switchable pages and 9 shortcuts per page, you get 90 fully customisable shortcuts in total. Organise them by app, task, or workflow and switch pages on the fly to stay in the zone.

Features

  • 90 fully customisable shortcuts — 10 pages × 9 per page
  • Assign any keyboard shortcut or key combination to any button
  • Switch pages on the fly to match your current task
  • USB plug-and-play — no drivers required
  • Compatible with Windows, macOS, and Linux
  • Configure shortcuts easily through the companion software

Frequently Asked Questions

Can't find what you're looking for? Contact us.

How many shortcuts can I create?
DigiMacroPad supports up to 90 shortcuts in total — 10 pages with 9 shortcuts each. Every shortcut is independently programmable, so you can have completely different actions on every page.
What kind of shortcuts can I assign?
You can assign any keyboard shortcut or key combination — copy (Ctrl+C), paste (Ctrl+V), undo, redo, save, screenshot, media controls, application shortcuts, and more. If your keyboard can do it, DigiMacroPad can trigger it.
How do I switch between pages?
Pages can be switched directly on the device with a dedicated button. You can organise pages by app or workflow — for example, one page for your browser, another for your code editor, and another for media controls.
Is it compatible with Windows and macOS?
Yes. DigiMacroPad presents itself as a standard USB HID keyboard, so it works on Windows, macOS, and Linux without any drivers. The companion configuration software runs on Windows and macOS.
How do I update the firmware?
Download the latest firmware from the Firmware tab. Hold the BOOT button while connecting DigiMacroPad via USB to enter bootloader mode, then use the included flashing tool to upload the .bin file. Your shortcut assignments are preserved after flashing.

HomePanel

A desktop home automation controller powered by ESP32 CYD. Control your entire home — lights, fans, blinds, TV, aircon, and more — through a touchscreen panel that integrates seamlessly with Home Assistant.

What is HomePanel?

HomePanel is a dedicated desktop home automation controller built on the ESP32 CYD (Cheap Yellow Display) — a compact, all-in-one board with a built-in touchscreen. It connects to your local network and integrates directly with Home Assistant, giving you a always-on physical control panel for your entire smart home.

No more reaching for your phone or shouting at a voice assistant. HomePanel sits on your desk or countertop and puts every automation at your fingertips — lights, fans, blinds, TV, aircon, and any other device connected to Home Assistant.

Features

  • Touchscreen control panel for all Home Assistant entities
  • Control lights, fans, blinds, TV, aircon, and more
  • Wi-Fi connected — integrates with your existing Home Assistant setup
  • Always-on display — instant access without unlocking a phone
  • Built on ESP32 CYD — compact, self-contained hardware
  • Web configuration — set up entities and layout from any browser
  • Open firmware — fully customisable for advanced users

What is ESP32 CYD?
ESP32 CYD (Cheap Yellow Display) is a compact development board that combines an ESP32 microcontroller with a built-in TFT touchscreen display. It provides Wi-Fi connectivity, a touchscreen interface, and all the processing power needed to run HomePanel — in a small, self-contained package.
Do I need Home Assistant to use HomePanel?
Yes. HomePanel is designed to work with Home Assistant, the open-source home automation platform. Your smart home devices must be connected to Home Assistant for HomePanel to control them. HomePanel communicates with Home Assistant over your local Wi-Fi network.
What devices can HomePanel control?
HomePanel can control any device exposed as an entity in Home Assistant — including lights, fans, blinds, covers, media players (TV), climate devices (aircon), switches, and more. If Home Assistant can control it, HomePanel can too.
How do I connect HomePanel to Home Assistant?
On first boot, HomePanel creates its own Wi-Fi hotspot. Connect to it from your phone or laptop, then enter your home Wi-Fi credentials and your Home Assistant URL and access token. HomePanel will join your network and connect to Home Assistant automatically on every subsequent boot.
How do I update the firmware?
Download the latest firmware from the Firmware tab. Hold the BOOT button while connecting HomePanel via USB to enter bootloader mode, then use the included flashing tool to upload the .bin file. Your entity configuration is preserved after flashing.
